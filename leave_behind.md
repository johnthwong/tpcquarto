The Tax Policy Center's tariff models are the most sophisticated model housed outside of a government agency. In addition to total tariff revenues, we estimate impacts on household incomes, businesses, and the broader economy. Our team is equipped to help state and local governments and stakeholders assess the impacts of tariffs on household incomes (nationally and by county, metropolitan statistical area, and state), regional economies (employment and output), and specific industries.

## How Our Model Compares
TPC's tariff models have several unique capabilities:

- Computes detailed tariff rates on each product from each country, by the day.
- Traces tariffs from intermediate imports, such as steel, to US-produced goods like automobiles and buildings.
- Models the impact of tariffs, and any remedial policies, on families across income and demographic spectrums, using hundreds of thousands of tax records.

## What We Can Produce for Cities
For each industry in your county, TPC can estimate how tariffs will affect:

- Output
- Employment
- Earnings
- Value-added

## What We Have Found
- 3 percent of all tariff burden fall on state and local government services.
- Tariffs makes construction more expensive, raising construction cost by one percent in real terms.
- Automobiles purchased by consumers are 5.6 percent more expensive due to current tariffs.
- In terms of distribution, tariffs behave like a flat tax, adding one percentage point to tax rates regardless of income.

## How Our Model Works
- Tariff Rules Engine
  Determine what is tariff rate on each import, which requires sophisticated modeling because of complex exemptions and ever-changing policies.
- Tariff Revenue Model: 
  Estimates how much buyers will reduce their demand for imports, which would affect the amount of tariffs paid.
- Supply Chain Model:
  Determines which industries end up paying these tariffs based on input-output data on what inputs each industry uses.
- Microsimulation Model:
  Analyzes impact at the household level, using detailed consumption and income data based on more than 200,000 tax records.
